The Right Way to Breathe

It sounds simple. Breathe right and you can relax.

There are two ways to breathe: through your chest or through your belly. When anxious, we tend to take quick, shallow breaths through our chests. This causes us to hyperventilate and makes the feelings of anxiety worse. Deep slow breathing works to counteract this using diaphragmatic breathing.

Inhale

To begin, you’ll take a long, slow breath through your nose. As you breathe in, push your belly out. Once your lungs are full, hold the breath for a moment.

To make sure you’re doing it right, place a hand on your belly and a hand on your chest. As you breathe in, the hand on your stomach should rise while the hand on your chest remains still.

Exhale

Next, you’ll exhale slowly through your mouth, pulling your belly in as you do so. Try to push all the air out.

Once done, pause for a few moments. You’ll repeat this cycle 8 times. As you grow more comfortable, adjust the breathing settings. There are many sound options and cycle lengths.
